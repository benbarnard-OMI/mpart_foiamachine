X. COMMON EXEMPTIONS (CANNOT REQUEST)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
✗ Individual patient medical records (HIPAA)
✗ Personal identifiable information (SSN, addresses)
✗ Ongoing criminal investigations
✗ Trade secrets/proprietary business info
✗ Attorney-client privileged communications
✗ Draft internal deliberative documents
✗ Information closed by federal law

✓ Can request de-identified aggregate data
✓ Can request with proper authorization/court order

XI. IF YOUR REQUEST IS DENIED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Step 1: Administrative Appeal
        - File with agency (typically 30-60 days)
        - Address appeal to agency head

Step 2: State FOIA Commission/Ombudsman (if available)
        - Free, informal resolution
        - Examples: IL Public Access Counselor, CT FOIA Commission

Step 3: Judicial Review
        - File lawsuit in state court
        - May recover attorney fees if you win
        - Agencies may face civil penalties

PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
Illinois:        $2,500-$5,000 per violation
Michigan:        $2,500-$7,500 per violation  
Virginia:        $1,000-$2,500 per violation
Pennsylvania:    Up to $1,500 per violation

XII. FEDERAL VS. STATE MEDICAID FOIA
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CMS (Federal):
- Use Federal FOIA (5 U.S.C. § 552)
- 20 working days response time
- Phone: (410) 786-5353
- Email: FOIA_Request@cms.hhs.gov
- Website: FOIA.gov portal

State Medicaid Agencies:
- Use STATE open records law
- Response time: 2-30 days (varies by state)
- Contact: State-specific FOIA officer
- See main spreadsheet for contacts

XIV. BEST PRACTICES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Before Submitting:
□ Check if records are already public on agency website
□ Call FOIA officer to confirm records exist
□ Review agency's FOIA procedures and fee schedule
□ Define specific time period and document types

Drafting Request:
□ Be specific - name documents/dates if possible
□ Request electronic format (cheaper, faster)
□ Ask for fee estimate if large request
□ Provide multiple contact methods
□ Cite correct state statute

After Submitting:
□ Keep copies of all correspondence
□ Note submission date and tracking number
□ Follow up if no response by deadline
□ Document any phone conversations
□ Track appeal deadlines (usually 30-60 days)

XV. U.S. TERRITORIES - SPECIAL NOTES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Puerto Rico, USVI, Guam, American Samoa, Northern Mariana Islands:

• Capped federal Medicaid funding (unlike states)
• FMAP: 76% (Puerto Rico) or 83% (others)
• May need to contact CMS Regional Office for federal records
• Follow local territory laws, not federal FOIA
• Response times vary - contact CMS regions:
  - Region 2 (PR, USVI): (212) 616-2519
  - Region 9 (Guam, AS, CNMI): (415) 744-3731

XVII. CRITICAL REMINDERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
✓ All requests must be in WRITING - no verbal requests accepted
✓ Check residency requirements - 8 states require state residency
✓ Be specific - vague requests will be denied or delayed
✓ Budget for fees - large requests can cost hundreds of dollars
✓ Know deadlines - response times vary from 2-30 days by state
✓ Appeal if denied - you have 30-60 days (varies by state)
✓ Use electronic format - faster and cheaper than paper
✓ Check online first - many records publicly available

✗ Cannot request individual patient records without authorization
✗ Cannot make overly broad requests ("all records")
✗ Cannot miss appeal deadlines - strictly enforced
✗ Cannot assume federal FOIA applies to state agencies
